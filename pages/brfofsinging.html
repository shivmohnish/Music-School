Carnatic Vocals

Carnatic vocal music is a classical Indian music tradition that involves singing compositions with precise rhythm, melody, and emotional expression. It emphasizes creativity and improvisation while adhering to the rules of ragas and talas. The term "Carnatic" is derived from the Sanskrit word "Karnataka," which historically encompassed present-day Tamil Nadu, Kerala, Andhra Pradesh, and parts of Karnataka. Carnatic music developed in this region, and its name reflects its strong association with the cultural and musical heritage of South India.

The major vocal forms or styles associated with Hindustani classical music are dhrupad, khyal, and tarana. Light classical forms include dhamar, trivat, chaiti, kajari, tappa, tap-khyal, thumri, dadra, ghazal and bhajan; these do not adhere to the rigorous rules of classical music.

Western Vocals

Western vocals refers to a style of singing that includes a variety of voice types, genres, and techniques. It is mainly influenced by music from Europe and North America. Western vocal music includes types like choral music sung by groups of singers often divided by voice, and musicals and operas, in which stories are told through singing. Other types are jazz and pop. This music is mainly from North America and Europe. Western vocal techniques consist of various styles like genres, styles, and techniques. Bollywood Vocals

If you want to sing as a hobby or learn to sing your favourite songs, this course is perfect for you. Our friendly teachers will help you with vocal training, and develop foundational skills for film music so you can sing your favorite songs with ease. Bollywood vocals are the singing style used in Indian films, which express emotions through songs. The singers who perform these songs are called playback singers. Playback singers record songs in a studio, and actors lip-sync to the pre-recorded songs on camera.
