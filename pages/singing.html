Indian singing is considered highly important as it serves as a profound cultural expression, deeply rooted in spirituality, connecting people to their heritage through the intricate system of "ragas" which evoke specific emotions, and is often used in religious ceremonies and celebrations, making it a vital part of Indian life and identity; it can be a powerful tool for personal development and connection with the divine.

Many Indian vocal compositions are dedicated to Hindu deities and are used in religious rituals, contributing to a sense of devotion and spiritual elevation.
